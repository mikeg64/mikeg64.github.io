\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Adds explicit thermal conduction term to the energy equation, d\+E/dt = Div(\+Q) 

where
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Q = kappa\+\_\+iso Grad(\+T) + kappa\+\_\+aniso(\mbox{[}b Dot Grad(\+T)\mbox{]}b) = heat flux
\item T = (P/d)$\ast$(mbar/k\+\_\+B) = temperature
\item b = magnetic field unit vector
\end{DoxyItemize}

Here
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item kappa\+\_\+iso is the isotropic coefficient of thermal diffusion
\item kappa\+\_\+aniso is the anisotropic coefficient of thermal diffusion
\end{DoxyItemize}

Note the kappa\textquotesingle{}s are D\+I\+F\+F\+U\+S\+I\+V\+I\+T\+I\+ES, not C\+O\+N\+D\+U\+C\+T\+I\+V\+I\+T\+I\+ES. Also note this version uses \char`\"{}dimensionless units\char`\"{} in that the factor (mbar/k\+\_\+B) is not included in calculating the temperature (instead, T=P/d is used). For cgs units, kappa must be entered in units of \mbox{[}cm$^\wedge$2/s\mbox{]}, and the heat fluxes would need to be multiplied by (k\+\_\+\+B/mbar).

The heat flux Q is calculated by calls to Heat\+Flux\+\_\+$\ast$ functions.
